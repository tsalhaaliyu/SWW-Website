INTRODUCTION

Guerrilla Warfare is a form of irregular warfare in which small groups of combatant/forces (para military, armed civilians) use military tactics
like ambushes, raid, sabotage, hit and run tactics and mobility to fight a larger and less mobile traditional forces/military. It’s also a military
or paramilitary operation conducted by irregular forces fighting on conventional affairs of the government. Guerrilla tactics focus on avoiding
head-on confrontations with enemy armies, instead engaged in limited skirmishes with the goal of exhausting adversaries and forcing them to
withdraw. Guerrilla groups often depend on the logistical and political support of either the local population or foreign sponsor/backers who do not engaged
in armed struggle but sympathize with the Guerrilla group efforts. Use of IED’s in the destruction of infrastructures.

    TYPES OF GUERRILLA WATFARE
  • Urban guerrilla: scatter and fight
  • Rural guerrilla: stand and fight

    • TACTICS AND STRATEGYS

    Tactics and Strategy: Tactically, guerrillas usually avoid confrontation with large units and formations of enemy troops but seek and
    attack small groups of enemy personnel and resources to gradually deplete the opposing force while minimizing their own losses. The guerrilla prizes
    mobility, secrecy, and surprise, organizing in small units and taking advantage of terrain that is difficult for larger unit to use. Strategically,
    Guerrilla Warfare is a type of asymmetric warfare, that is, competition between opponent of unequal strength and they
    aim not simply to defeat an enemy, but to win popular support and political influence to the enemy’s cost. They aims to magnify the
    impact of a small, mobile force on a larger one when they won, they weaken their enemy by attrition, eventually forcing them to withdraw.
