DEFINITION

Infiltration is the tactical maneuver of troops from friendly area to hostile area by stealth with the aim of accomplishing a mission or task. (Exfiltration) or attacking and holding the ground. An attack of this nature will depend heavily on the element of surprise and may force the enemy to fight in an unexpected and unfavourable area.

PURPOSE OF INFILTRATION

  1. To filter unseen through forward position order to teach reach objectives on infiltration troop may wish to.
  2. Seize a piece of ground of ladical importance behind forward position so as to isolate or ambush the enemy.
  3. Attack a key position in depth. Possibly while forward position of the enemy are attacked simultaneously.
  4. To destroy a strong hold of enemy
  5. To destroy or neutralize selected enemy location such as gun areas, high grounds. Communication centres power supply.
  6. To seize important tactical feature such as bridges high ground and communication centre.
  7. To disrupt and delay or hold the movement of enemy supporting units and reserves
  8. To stage a deception for operation in other areas.
