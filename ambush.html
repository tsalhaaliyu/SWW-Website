Definition of Ambush

Ambush can be defined as a surprise attack by a force lying in wait upon a moving or temporarily halted enemy. It is usually a brief encounter and does not require the capture and holding of ground.

Types of Ambush

  1. Opportunity Ambush
  2. Deliberate Ambush
  1. Opportunity Ambush

    2. This is the ambush that has no time enough for planning. There is little time for rehearsals. It comes in a situation where enemy movement is detected.
    his is an ambush forced upon a Commander by circumstances. This ambush is usually sprung by a fighting patrol.

  2. Deliberate Ambush

    3. This is the type of ambush that is based on thorough, intelligent and detailed planning. The force employed for springing such an ambush is basically
    a fighting patrol composed of selected men and a leader. The method of deliberate ambush include:

    Under delibrate Ambush.

    • Short term ambush.
    • Long term ambush.
    • Manned ambush.
    • Unmanned.
    • Linear Ambush
    • Area of Ambush

Short term ambush

Short Term Ambush: This is an ambush planned and executed within l2hrs. In this case, the troops will be in the ambush site until the presence of the enemy
is noticed. If the enemy does not show up, then they can withdraw.

Long term Ambush

Long Term Ambush: This is the type of ambush which is deliberately planned and lasts more than 24hrs; sometimes it can last for days. In this case, an ambush
site is developed and occupied effectively by troops taking part. Regular relief should be ensured throughout the duration of the ambush to reduce fatigue.

Manned Ambush

This is the type of Ambush planned on the consist of men , is the type Ambush that is physically planed by troops.

Unmanned Ambush

This is type of ambush does not involve the occupation of the ambush site physically by troops. This ambush is carried using bobby traps, explosives and land mines.

Area of Ambush

Area of Ambush. is used to cover several killing areas when it is uncertain which route the enemy will use or from which direction he will appear.

Linear Ambush

Linear Ambush. In this type of ambush the gun group (killer group) is concentrated on one killing area, in extended line formation. It is the form employed
most frequently on tracks, roads and rivers.

Conduct/Stages of Ambush

The following are stages of ambush:

  1. Recce/preparatory stage
  2. Occupation stage.
  3. Springing/execution stage.
  4. Withdrawal stage.

  • Recce/preparatory stage

    • Recce/Preparatory Stage. This is the preparatory stage. Map recce of the ambush site or ground recce (if possible) should be conducted. After the
    recce, clear briefing must prepared and delivered. Selection of site and position of the groups are clearly stated and interpreted to the all groups.
    All information needed about the adversary must be clearly understood by the ambush party.

  • Occupation stage.

    • Occupation. We have the T shape and V shape method of occupation. The occupation begins immediately after the confirmatory recce party has returned.
    At the dispersal point (which we may call Hide Out1 RV or Commando Base), the 3mbush party breaks into their. Various groups and move to occupy the ambush site from the near.

    Springing/execution stage.

    Springing/Execution Stage. The signal for springing must be given by the Commander. Normally the Commander fires the first short to alert the groups.
    Remember that the early warning group must give the early warning of the adversary approach either by use of twine of any other means that will not
    reveal the ambush position to the adversary.

  • Withdrawal stage.

    • Withdrawal stage. After, the commander orders the ceasefire, there must be searching (if need be) before withdrawal. The withdrawal must be by groups
    and normally the protection group withdraws last. Signal for withdrawal must be clearly given by the Commander to avoid confusion. The ambush party
    withdraws to a pre determined RV to avoid en counter attack or mortar

    Composition of Ambush Party/Task

    The following groups are involved in an ambush:

    1. Early warning groups (left and right).
      Their task is to give early warning and to alert the commander of the adversary approach.
    2. Cut off groups left and right,
      their task is to cut enemy who is try to escape from killing zone.
    3. killer group or Assult group
      their task is to spring the ambush and courste maximum casuality on the enemy.
    4. Protection group left and right
      their task is to protet side from enemy infiltration .
    5. Reserve group
      their task is to give re-enforcement to any of the parties that fall casualty and also act as casualty evacuation.

    Principle of Ambush

      To achieve a successful ambush, the following principles must be adhered to, Good Information.

    • Basic essential of good information and 100% of surprise.
    • Simplicity.
      The appreciation, plan and briefing must be clear if as possible, giving complete detailed orders.
    • Execution.
      All mission must be broke down into phases at all time. All troops must have perfect battle discipline.
    • Self Discipline.
      All troops must have self discipline. The commander can’t be everywhere, either can he see the whole groups. Therefore individuals are responsible for their own fire positions, camouflage and concealment and all must be determined to kill the enemy. There must be no smoking,
      coughing unnecessarily or laughing.
    • Endurance.
      All troops must be at alert observant for a long period of time. All must have the ability to endure even when the going gets tough.
    • Fire Control.
      There must be maximum use of fire power on the enemy. All troops must listen to the commander’s fire control orders. All must have ability to shoot to kill
    • Sitting.
      An ambush area needs to be sited carefully to allow all of the adversary to enter the area and prevent them from escaping.
    • Spring.
      Orders must be clearly spelt out such as when to spring and by whom the ambush will be sprung. The signal for opening fire must be been given clearly
    • Cease Fire.
      The order for cease fire must be given from the commander.
    • Administration.
      Good administration is very important eg supply of arms and ammunition, service support such as water,equipment and first aid.

    Reason of Ambush

    1. To delay enemy advance.
    2. To destroy the enemy.
    3. To capture prisoners of war.(POW)
    4. To gain information about enemy.
    5. To capture or destroy adversary’s equipment.
    6. To divert adversary’s attention.
    7. To prevent the enemy from accomplishing their mission.
    8. To delay the enemy from approaching own position.
    9. To demoralise the enemy.
    10. To inflict maximum casualty on the enemy.

    SELECTION OF AMBUSH SITE

    1. Selecting an ambush site, the following points must be borne in mind:
    2. The size and type of target to be ambushed.
    3. Method of movement of the target eg whether they are on foot or mounted.
    4. Time of movement of the adversary eg whether it is a day or night movement.
    5. Composition and position of the adversary guards or escorts.
    6. Does the adversary have reinforcement capabilities and other protective arrangements?
